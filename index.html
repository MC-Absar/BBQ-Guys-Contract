⏳ Grace Time & Service Timeline▼
Please allow a grace time of 30–45 minutes in your service time as the food we prepare is LIVE. Our team works hard to serve fresh, high-quality halal food and timing may vary slightly to ensure the best experience for your guests.

We ask that you treat our staff with dignity and respect. Any concerns should be directed calmly to our team lead on site. The BBQ Guys Chicago reserves the right to cancel services if this clause is violated through unreasonable pressure or disrespectful conduct.

  • The catering team requires venue access at least 2 hours before food service begins for setup
  • Client must provide a full event timeline at least 2 days before the event
  • Overtime beyond 2 hours of food service is billed at $200/hr
🍽️ 4. Scope of Catering Services▼

The BBQ Guys Chicago ("Caterer") agrees to provide the following services for the above event(s):

  • Live halal BBQ and/or buffet catering services as specified in the confirmed menu selection (Section 2)
  • Professional setup and breakdown of catering stations at the agreed venue
  • Serving equipment, chafing dishes, and necessary utensils for buffet service (unless otherwise agreed)
  • Food preparation and cooking to Halal standards as certified — no pork or non-Halal meat products will be served
  • Coordination with the venue manager or event coordinator regarding setup logistics
  • One (1) pre-event planning call to finalize guest count, menu, and setup requirements
  • Final confirmation of all details no later than 2 days before the event
Service Limitations:
  • Tableware (plates, napkins, cups) and disposables are not included unless specifically added as a paid extra
  • Serving staff beyond the standard catering crew is subject to an additional staffing fee
  • Bar service, alcohol, and non-halal items are not provided under any circumstances
📦 5. Pricing▼

Pricing is custom per event based on guest count, menu selections, and service requirements. Your per-person rate is confirmed at the time of booking and outlined in this agreement.

❌ 6. Cancellation & Rescheduling Policy▼
Cancellation by Client:
  • More than 60 days before event: Non-refundable retainer is forfeited; no additional fee.
  • 31–60 days before event: 50% of the total contract value is due in addition to the forfeited retainer.
  • 30 days or fewer before event: 100% of the total contract value is due. Food costs and labor already incurred are non-negotiable.
Rescheduling by Client:

One (1) complimentary rescheduling is permitted if the new date is available and written notice is given at least 30 days in advance. Any subsequent reschedule is subject to a $150 rescheduling fee. Rescheduling within 14 days of the event is treated as a cancellation under the applicable cancellation tier.

Cancellation by Caterer:

In the unlikely event The BBQ Guys Chicago must cancel, a full refund of all payments received will be issued and the Caterer will make reasonable efforts to provide a qualified referral or alternative catering arrangement.

✅ 7. Client Responsibilities▼

To ensure a smooth and successful catering experience, the Client agrees to the following:

  • Provide a final confirmed guest count no later than 2 days before the event. Guest count increases are accepted up to 2 days before, subject to availability. Guest count decreases must be requested at least 14 days before the event; decreases of more than 15% within 14 days will not result in a reduction of contract fees.
  • Ensure the Caterer has access to the venue, including kitchen or prep area, electricity, and running water access. A water hose with outdoor access is required for live BBQ setups — please confirm availability with your venue in advance.
  • Provide accurate venue contact information and any applicable vendor load-in requirements, elevator access codes, or parking details at least 7 days prior.
  • Communicate all confirmed dietary restrictions, allergies, and Halal requirements clearly in the pre-event call.
  • Designate a day-of point of contact who is reachable throughout the event to coordinate with the catering team.
  • Ensure the venue permits live cooking and/or open-flame BBQ if applicable; any venue permits required for live fire cooking are the Client's responsibility to obtain.
👥 8. Guest Count & Event Scale▼
Final confirmed guest count is due 2 days prior to your event date. Increases are accepted up to 2 days before, subject to availability. Decreases must be requested at least 14 days before the event — decreases within 14 days do not reduce the contract fee.

The Client agrees to disclose a reasonable and accurate estimate of expected guest attendance at the time of booking. If the actual guest count exceeds the confirmed estimate by more than 20% on the day of the event, the Caterer reserves the right to:

  • Adjust the total fee proportionally based on the actual guest count at the contracted per-person rate
  • Limit portions or modify the menu to accommodate the available food supply if advance notice was not provided
  • Invoice the Client for the overage within 48 hours of the event at the contracted per-person rate

Events exceeding 150 guests are subject to custom pricing, additional staffing fees, and extended setup time. These must be discussed and agreed upon in writing at least 21 days before the event.

🌤️ 9. Outdoor Events & Weather▼

For events held fully or partially outdoors, the Client is responsible for providing adequate shelter, shade, and weather protection for the catering team and equipment. This includes a covered tent or canopy structure sufficient to protect grills, food stations, and staff from precipitation and direct sun.

The Caterer reserves the right to pause or restructure service if outdoor conditions become unsafe or unworkable, including but not limited to extreme heat (above 95°F), heavy rain, lightning, or high winds. No refund will be issued for weather-related service interruptions beyond the Caterer's control.

Day-of Weather Force Majeure: If severe weather (tornado warning, flash flood emergency, or equivalent government-issued alert) forces a venue change or cancellation within 24 hours of the event, The BBQ Guys Chicago will make best efforts to reschedule at no additional rescheduling fee. If rescheduling is not possible, a credit of 50% of payments made (excluding the non-refundable retainer) will be applied toward a future booking within 12 months.

If the venue requires open-flame permits for live BBQ cooking, those permits are the Client's sole responsibility to obtain and provide to the Caterer at least 7 days before the event.

☪ 10. Food Safety & Halal Compliance▼

The BBQ Guys Chicago is committed to providing fully Halal-compliant food service. All meats sourced and prepared by the Caterer are Halal-certified. The Caterer does not serve pork, pork-derived products, alcohol, or any non-Halal items at any event.

The Client is responsible for ensuring that no outside food or ingredients are introduced to the catering setup that could compromise Halal compliance. The Caterer is not responsible for cross-contamination caused by outside food brought to the event by guests, the Client, or other vendors.

The Caterer complies with applicable local health and food safety codes. Food will be held at safe serving temperatures per USDA food safety guidelines. The Caterer is not responsible for food-borne illness resulting from food held beyond the recommended serving window (generally 2 hours at room temperature or 1 hour above 90°F).

Leftover Food Policy:

We will only leave items that are cooked and left unconsumed by guests. We will not cook additional food for leftovers or carryout, nor will we leave behind uncooked food items. The Client assumes full responsibility for any leftovers left behind after service ends.

Allergy Disclaimer:

The Client is responsible for communicating all guest food allergies and dietary restrictions prior to the event. While the Caterer will make reasonable accommodations, The BBQ Guys Chicago operates in a kitchen that handles common allergens including nuts, dairy, gluten, shellfish, and eggs, and cannot guarantee an allergen-free environment.

🔄 Menu Cost & Substitution Policy▼

All menu prices are subject to change within 14 days of your event due to market fluctuations. If a significant change in ingredient cost is established, you will have two options:

  • Option 1: Accept the updated cost while maintaining your current menu based on current market prices
  • Option 2: Accept a comparable substitute menu item at the same per-person price

Note: Menu items are subject to availability and may need to be changed if unavailable. We will notify the Client as early as possible if any substitution is required.

💳 11. Payment Terms & Late Fees▼
Non-Refundable Retainer:

A non-refundable $200 retainer is due at the time of booking to secure your event date.

Final Payment:

Final payment is due before the catering team departs the event. The BBQ Guys Chicago reserves the right to request full payment at any time during the event if the Client, their guests, or any third party exhibits disrespectful or troublesome behavior toward our team.

Accepted Payment Methods:

Cash · Zelle · Check (payable to The BBQ Guys Chicago). All prices are based on cash/check purchase.

Chargeback Protection:

The Client agrees not to initiate a chargeback or payment dispute without first contacting The BBQ Guys Chicago directly. Any fraudulent chargeback will result in pursuit of the full contract value plus applicable legal fees.

Late Payment:
  • Remaining balance not received by the end of the event date will incur a one-time late fee of 5% of the outstanding balance (minimum $50). An additional 2% per week will accrue for each week the balance remains unpaid.
  • If the balance remains unpaid 72 hours before the event, The BBQ Guys Chicago reserves the right to withhold services until payment is received in full.
  • Failure to pay does not entitle the Client to a refund of the retainer or release from contractual obligations.
  • Returned checks will be charged a $35 returned check fee in addition to any outstanding balance.

Accepted payment methods: Cash | Zelle | Check (payable to The BBQ Guys Chicago). All payments are final and non-reversible once processed.

✈️ 12. Travel & Distance Fee▼

The BBQ Guys Chicago serves events in the Chicago, IL metropolitan area. Standard travel fees apply:

  • Events within 30 miles of Chicago, IL: Included in the base contract fee
  • 31–60 miles from base city: $100 travel fee
  • 61–100 miles from base city: $200 travel fee
  • Over 100 miles or out-of-area: Travel fee to be mutually agreed upon in writing prior to contract signing and added as a signed addendum

Out-of-state events require a written addendum signed by both parties confirming travel logistics, accommodations, and any additional fees before the booking is finalized.

The BBQ Guys Chicago assumes no responsibility for any damage or loss of merchandise, alcohol, equipment, furniture, clothing, or other valuables prior to, during, or after the event.

I, the Client, understand that by hosting an event at my home or venue, accidents, breakage, and damage may occur. I will not bill, charge, or sue The BBQ Guys Chicago for any resulting damages.

Equipment Damage:

Any damage to The BBQ Guys Chicago equipment, grills, or supplies caused by guests, venue staff, or third parties is the Client's financial responsibility. The Client agrees to reimburse The BBQ Guys Chicago within 14 days of the event.

Limitation of Liability:

The BBQ Guys Chicago's total liability to the Client under this agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid. The Caterer shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including but not limited to loss of enjoyment, emotional distress, or reputational harm.

The Caterer is expressly not responsible for:

  • Conduct, behavior, or actions of guests during the event
  • Acts of God, extreme weather, natural disasters, public health emergencies, or government-mandated restrictions
  • Outcomes resulting from incomplete, inaccurate, or late communication of event details by the Client
🌪️ 14. Force Majeure▼

Neither party shall be held liable for failure to perform any obligation under this agreement resulting from circumstances beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God, extreme weather, natural disasters, pandemic, epidemic, government-imposed restrictions, civil unrest, power outages, or supply chain disruptions affecting food availability.

In the event of force majeure, The BBQ Guys Chicago will make every reasonable effort to fulfill contracted services or propose an alternative. If the event is cancelled due to force majeure, the non-refundable retainer shall be retained by the Caterer as compensation for reserved capacity and preparation. Any payments beyond the retainer will be refunded on a pro-rated basis based on costs already incurred.

The BBQ Guys Chicago reserves the right to immediately cease services without refund if any member of the catering team is subjected to verbal abuse, threats, harassment, or physically unsafe conditions by the Client, their guests, or any third party at the event. The safety and dignity of our team members are non-negotiable.

The Caterer also reserves the right to refuse any request to serve non-Halal items, alcohol, or food items not agreed upon in this contract, regardless of instruction from the Client or event planner on the day of the event.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions▼
Do you provide plates, napkins, and cutlery?

Standard packages do not include disposable tableware. If you need plates, napkins, cups, and cutlery, this can be added as a paid extra — please ask at the time of booking.

What if my guest count changes after signing?

Final guest count is due 2 days before your event. Increases are accepted up to 2 days before, subject to availability. Decreases must be requested at least 14 days before — decreases within 14 days do not reduce the contract fee.

How long does setup take?

Our team requires venue access at least 2 hours before food service begins. Please coordinate with your venue to ensure this window is available. For very large events (200+ guests), 3 hours may be needed.

Is your food certified Halal?

Yes. All meats used by The BBQ Guys Chicago are sourced from certified Halal suppliers. We do not serve pork, pork-derived products, or alcohol at any event under any circumstances.

Can we make menu changes after signing?

Menu change requests must be submitted at least 14 days before your event. We cannot guarantee availability of new items with less notice. Substitutions due to market unavailability may occur — we'll always notify you promptly.

What happens if we need to reschedule?

One complimentary reschedule is permitted with at least 30 days written notice, subject to availability. A second reschedule incurs a $150 fee. Rescheduling within 14 days is treated as a cancellation.

How do I pay the retainer?

The $200 non-refundable retainer is due immediately after signing. Send payment via Zelle (contact us for details), cash, or check payable to The BBQ Guys Chicago. Include your name and event date in the payment note.

Will you tag us on social media?

We love to share our work! With your permission (Section 19), we'll tag @thebbqguyschicago on any photos or videos from your event. You can opt out or limit sharing — just let us know before the event.

💰 Gratuity Policy▼

Gratuity is not included in the contract price and is entirely at the Client's discretion. It is never expected or required. If you feel our team delivered exceptional service, gratuity is always deeply appreciated and shared equally among the entire catering crew.

Suggested gratuity range: 10–15% of the food subtotal for exceptional service. This can be provided in cash to the team lead at the end of the event.

Gratuity does not affect service quality. Every client receives our full effort and dedication regardless of whether gratuity is provided.
